# High-register editing

**Plan:** §3.2, §5.2, §11.17 · **DR:** DR-15

High-register language is not a defect.

## Protect

- Corpus- or glossary-attested precise diction and aliases
- Domain-specific Latinate or technical terms when accurate
- Complex syntax that carries real qualifications

## Do not

- Simplify solely because a term is formal, uncommon, or demanding
- Apply universal bans on em dashes, semicolons, anaphora, parallelism, long sentences, or passive voice
- Confuse ornamental inflation with precision

## Edit only when

The term or construction is inaccurate, redundant, unsupported, or externally imposed AI residue **and** evidence supports the claim.

Paired fixtures must show: precise corpus/glossary diction survives; ornamental inflation can be removed with evidence.
